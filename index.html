Ahead of Tuesday: February and March billing
Short version: Across February and March combined, Hugo delivered above billed overall — net +781 hrs (+0.7%), carried by oversupply on Lilly SA (+8.1%) and Lagos (+7.3%). At a workflow level, Gastro and Missouri came in below billed in both months — Gastro -11.0% combined (buffer cut too thin to absorb PTO and absences) and Missouri -3.9% combined (attrition ~2x our launch assumption). Both drivers are on us, both being fixed. Below: a direct answer to what was raised Friday, per-workflow numbers on the correct baseline, and what's changing.
Addressing the three things raised Friday
"Hugo might be billing for absenteeism"
Hugo's billing method has been top-down against contracted capacity, rooted in MSA language. Our operating assumption was that Assembled scheduled already reflected PTO shrinkage — under that assumption, all eight workflow-months would have landed above contracted, which is what drove billing at contract with the rest absorbed by Hugo. We should have validated that assumption with Cris and your WFM team. We didn't, and that's on us. On the correct baseline, delivered across the two months combined came in above billed (+1.3%), carried by oversupply on SA and Lagos. Gastro and Missouri came in below billed in both months though — see drivers below.
"Hugo wasn't prepared to show how they bill"
Fair, and that's on us. This portal walks every billed hour from invoice to individual agent, by workflow. From the next invoice forward, a portal like this ships with every bill by the 5th of the following month.
"No raw data attached to invoices"
Also fair. Every hour in the portal is pulled from raw sources (Assembled for production, Penmac for Missouri payroll, our internal billing model, training records). Every agent listed by name. Going forward raw data ships with every invoice, and the invoice basis itself moves to bottom-up actuals so there's no ambiguity about what's billed vs what's delivered.

Combined across both months: SA +2,559 hrs (+8.1%), Lagos +1,884 hrs (+7.3%), MO -1,526 hrs (-3.9%), Gastro -2,139 hrs (-11.0%). Net +781 hrs (+0.7%) above billed across the engagement. Two drivers where we came up short, both on us, both being fixed.
Driver 1: Gastro buffer was cut too thin
As part of a Q4 2025 efficiency push, Gastro buffer was taken from our standard ~15% down to ~4% (47 actual agents against 45 contracted). At 15% buffer, PTO and absences absorb into the cushion and delivered comfortably exceeds billed. At 4%, they don't. That's the structural reason Gastro came in below billed in both February (-1,191 hrs) and March (-949 hrs). Feb and March numbers both reflect comp-day schedule hours removed per the correct treatment — 328 hrs Feb and 440 hrs March, across 13 and 18 named agents respectively. Buffer is being restored to our 10-15% standard this week (commitment #3).
Driver 2: Missouri attrition and hiring profile
Missouri attrition came in ~2x our launch assumption: 134 active in March against 175 contracted. Root cause is hiring approach and hiring profile. GMC Springfield's recruiting engine was built around sales roles with a different profile than CX work demands. We already billed MO at actual-performing headcount rather than full contract, but attended delivery from those agents still landed below billed in both months because attrition cut further into attended hours.
The recruiting overhaul is already underway: different sourcing channels outside the standard Springfield sales pipeline, shift to experienced CX hires over entry-level, remote backup capacity to augment in-center during attrition spikes, broader hiring profile aligned to our SA and Lagos CX benchmarks. Hugo Mayers is leading this. Separate conversation with your team next week on specifics.
What's changing, going forward
1
Bottom-up billing on all future invoices
Raw data actuals become the invoice basis starting next cycle. Delivered equals billed by construction. MSA top-down language continues to be what our billing team uses to double-check numbers against contracted capacity, but the invoice itself is built from raw data.
2
Weekly reconciliation flowing into monthly invoice packets
Starting this week: raw data recon weekly, rolling into the monthly invoice packet. Once the cadence is stable and both teams are confident in numbers, we taper to biweekly then monthly. A weekly audit beat at the start is good practice for rebuilding visibility.
3
Gastro buffer restored to standard
Hugo's operating standard is 10-15% buffer above contracted on steady-state workflows. We broke our own standard on Gastro at the end of 2025 and that's what showed up in Feb and March. Buffer is being restored to 10-15% this week.
4
Missouri recruiting overhaul, underway now
New sourcing channels, broader hiring profile aligned to SA and Lagos CX benchmarks, experienced hires over entry-level, remote backup. Separate conversation with your team next week.
5
Training visibility in Assembled (request back to your team)
Today Assembled tracks production only, so training hours do not have the same visibility as production hours. If your team enables training-workflow tracking in Assembled, it closes a real reporting gap for both sides.
FYI on the specific prorating method Cris and Ross walked through Friday. We agree with the baseline for "delivered" — scheduled minus PTO minus absences plus training, TL, and other billed — which is the baseline the numbers above use. Where we differ is the prorating step itself (delivered × contracted/billable). Prorating assumes agents are "named" to contracted versus buffer slots, but Hugo staffs all 47 Gastro agents as cross-trained and interchangeable; they produce the same work product that your team accepted. That said, this is the debate bottom-up makes moot — delivered equals billed by construction from next invoice forward. Happy to walk through on Tuesday if useful.

Key terms & billing definitions
Maximum contracted hours
Max hours Hugo can bill. Billable agents × working days × hrs/day.
Training hours
Hours in ~2-week pre-production training. NOT tracked in Assembled.
Production hours
Hours contracted agents spend in production after training. Tracked in Assembled.
Total invoiced hours
Training (contracted) + Production (contracted) + TLs + Other + OT. What the client pays for.
Assembled hours
Hours in Assembled (WFM tool). Production only. Includes contracted AND buffer. Includes overtime.
Buffer agents
Extra agents for SLA coverage. Never invoiced. Production appears in Assembled. Hugo absorbs cost.
Scheduled (adjusted)
Total Hours from Assembled MINUS PTO, LOA, holiday, vacation. The "paid-for production time" number.
Attended
Scheduled MINUS same-day absences (NCNS, late arrival, early departure). Hours agents actually worked.
Productive
Attended MINUS breaks, meals, meetings, coaching, readiness. Hours on-task delivering value.
Overtime hours
Extra hours requested by client. Billed at ~20% premium.
Weekend hours
Weekend shifts billed at 1.2x premium. Added from March 2026 after compliance review.
Completed training
Training hours billed to GH reflect agents who completed training. Drop-outs are not invoiced.
